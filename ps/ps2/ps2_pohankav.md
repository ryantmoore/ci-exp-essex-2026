### Exercise 3.1

a)  The standard deviation is a quantity derived from the population mean and how far away, on average, individual observations are from that mean. It is calculated as $$\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}(x_i - X)^2}$$ for the population, where x_i denotes one observation, X the population mean, and N the number of observations. For the standard deviation of a sample, we substract 1 from N in the fraction denominator and use the sample mean.  

The standard error is a measure of uncertainty of an estimated statistic, or the standard deviation of a sampling distribution. For example, we can calculate the standard error of a difference-in-means estimator in an experiment or the standard error of a beta coefficient in a linear regression.
